## What is a Product Page Offer?

A Product Page Offer is an upsell or cross-sell presented directly on the single product page, typically just below the "Add to Cart" button. It encourages the customer to bundle additional, related items with the main product before they even reach the cart.

These offers often look like "Frequently Bought Together" bundles or simple checkbox add-ons.

## How it Works

1. **Contextual Evaluation:** When a customer views a product page, UpsellBay checks if there are any active offers specifically targeted at that product or category.
2. **Native Placement:** The offer is seamlessly injected near the native WooCommerce add-to-cart form, feeling like a built-in feature of your theme.
3. **Bundled Action:** Customers can select the add-on. When they click the main "Add to Cart" button, both the primary product and the selected offer are added to the cart simultaneously.
4. **Smart Redundancy Prevention:** UpsellBay automatically prevents an offer from showing on its own product page. You don't need to configure complex exclusion rules to prevent a product from offering itself!

## The Sales Psychology

Product Page Offers leverage **Social Proof** and **Bundled Value**.

- **Anticipating Needs:** By offering batteries for a flashlight or a memory card for a camera, you are anticipating a future friction point and solving it immediately.
- **The "Frequently Bought Together" Effect:** Amazon popularized this. It uses social proof implicitly—"Other smart people bought these together, so you should too."
- **Perceived Value:** Presenting the offer as a bundle (e.g., "Add the cleaning kit for 15% off") shifts the customer's mindset from "Should I buy this?" to "Look at the deal I'm getting."

## What Can Go Wrong

- **Decision Paralysis:** Offering too many choices (e.g., 5 different add-ons with complex variations) can overwhelm the customer, causing them to abandon the main purchase entirely.
- **Visual Clutter:** If the offer takes up too much vertical space, it pushes important product descriptions or reviews below the fold, hurting the overall conversion rate.
- **Pricing Mismatch:** Offering an add-on that is more expensive than the base product rarely works at this stage, as the customer hasn't yet committed to the base purchase.

## How This Helps Your Business

By capturing the customer's interest early in the journey, Product Page Offers consistently increase the items-per-order metric. They are excellent for moving high-margin, low-cost accessories that customers might otherwise buy from a competitor later.
